Cloud Computing


A week ago I was asked if any specific technology new/recent catches my attention. With little thought, I blurted out "cloud computing", then quickly found my lack of knowledge to be the reason this technology piqued my interest.


About a year ago I started to notice the concept of clouds and it's many uses. Dreamforce (2014) planted a root of knowledge that has never been watered. Until I discover the inner working that make up this magnificent concept (blog post coming soon), here are two simple concepts I've experienced so far:

  • Cloud Storage Services:
    In my previous job I was expected to facilitate a large amount of records regarding quarterly orders from clients. With ignorance of IT back in the day, how else would I be able to store and retrieve secure information from multiple locations all over the world than with a cloud platform.
    Large corporations are trusting services from cloud computing companies to hold their data and ensure simple and secure access from multiple locations. Example of Companies that offer these services: Apple iCloud, Amazon Cloud Drive, DropBox, Google Drive.
  • Deployment Services:
    I assume most of you reading this are aware of this concept. Companies are finding a demand for web deployment services. These are cloud platforms that manage your website and even data storage required for functionality. Some examples of cloud deployment services are: Heroku, AWS, Microsoft Azure.

While the concept of cloud computing has been around since the 1970s, it wasn't until the new millennium that this concept started to pioneer, resulting in a large demand from companies small and large. It may not be a completly new technology, but has how shown recent growth and success, and has been widely accepted into business culture.
